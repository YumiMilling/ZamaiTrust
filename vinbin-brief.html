The Problem

Good Trucks. Wasted Trips.

Every day in Zambia, trucks run half-empty. A load goes one direction. The return trip is dead kilometres — fuel burned, tyres worn, driver paid, nothing earned. Or you find a load last minute through a broker who takes a cut without adding any value.

Shippers have the same problem from the other side: they need a truck, they make ten phone calls, they're not sure if the driver is reliable, and they hand over cash with no guarantee.

The Solution

How ZamaLoad Works

Simple. Direct. No broker in the middle.

1
Post Your Truck

You have a truck going Choma to Lusaka on Thursday with 15 tonnes available. You post it: route, date, capacity, your rate. Takes 2 minutes.

2
Shipper Books & Pays

A shipper sees your listing, books the load, and pays into a secure account. The money is held — not in your pocket yet, but guaranteed. You know it's real.

3
Deliver & Get Paid

You deliver the goods. Shipper confirms receipt on the app. Payment releases to you immediately. No chasing. No delays. No disputes.

"No broker. No phone tag. You post your truck, someone books it, you deliver, you get paid."

Why This. Why Now.

Zambia Needs This

Road freight is the backbone of this economy. Rail is unreliable. Air is expensive. Everything moves by truck — maize, fertiliser, goods, building materials, produce. The informal system of WhatsApp groups and phone brokers is the only thing holding it together right now.

ZamaLoad is being built by a Zambia-based team who understand the roads, the operators, and the shippers. This is not a foreign app being adapted. It is being designed from Batoka, Southern Province, for Zambian conditions.

We are starting with owner-operators — people with one or two trucks, who make every decision themselves, and who feel every empty kilometre personally.
